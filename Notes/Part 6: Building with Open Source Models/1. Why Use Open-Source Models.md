# Why Use Open Source AI Models?

## Why not just use hosted models like OpenAI or Anthropic?

While hosted models are powerful and convenient, open source models offer several important advantages depending on the use case.

## 1. Cost Savings

- Hosted AI models charge based on the number of tokens used.
- As usage increases, the cost can grow significantly.
- Open source models can be:
    - Run locally for free (after the initial hardware setup).
    - Deployed on your own servers.
- This gives complete control over infrastructure costs.

## 2. Privacy

- When running an open source model locally, **no data leaves your environment**.
- Prompts and responses remain on your own machine or servers.
- This is especially important for applications involving sensitive data, such as:
    - Healthcare
    - Finance
    - Government projects

## 3. Flexibility

- Open source models do not lock you into a single provider.
- You have access to **hundreds or even thousands of different models**.
- Many available models are **Small Language Models (SLMs)** that are:
    - Optimized for specific tasks.
    - Sometimes able to outperform large commercial LLMs for those specialized tasks.

## 4. Offline Access

- Hosted models require an internet connection.
- Open source models can run **completely offline**.
- This makes them suitable for:
    - Edge devices
    - Field work
    - Environments with limited or unreliable internet connectivity

## 5. Transparency

- With open source models, you can inspect:
    - The source code.
    - In some cases, the training data.
- This helps you:
    - Understand how the model works.
    - Identify potential sources of bias.
    - Gain better visibility into the model's behavior.

## Summary

Open source AI models provide several key benefits over hosted models:

- Cost savings by avoiding per-token pricing.
- Better privacy since data stays within your own environment.
- Greater flexibility with access to a wide variety of models, including specialized Small Language Models.
- Offline capability without requiring internet access.
- Increased transparency through access to the model's code and, sometimes, its training data.
