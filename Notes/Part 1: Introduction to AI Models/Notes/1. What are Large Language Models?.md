## What is a Large Language Model (LLM)?

- A **Language Model** is a system trained to understand and generate human language.

### Examples of LLMs

**Commercial Models**

- OpenAI GPT
- Google Gemini
- Anthropic Claude
- xAI Grok

**Open-Source Models**

- Llama
- Mistral

### Why are they called "Large"?

They are trained on massive amounts of data, including:

- Books
- Articles
- Forums
- Documentation
- Source code
- Websites

Through training, they learn:

- Grammar
- Sentence structure
- Writing styles
- Common facts
- Language patterns

## How LLMs Generate Responses

- LLMs do **not look up answers** like a search engine.

Instead, they:

- Predict the most likely next words based on patterns learned during training.
- Work similarly to autocomplete, but at a much larger scale.

### Example

Prompt:

> "The capital of France is"

Model prediction:

> "Paris"

The model predicts this because it has seen similar patterns many times during training.

## What Exists Inside an LLM?

An LLM is essentially:

- A huge mathematical model
- Often several gigabytes in size
- Contains billions of parameters (another reason they are called **large**)

### Parameters Represent

- Language patterns
- Facts
- Grammar rules
- Tone and style
- Relationships between words

### Important Fact

LLMs:

- Do not think
- Do not understand language like humans
- Do not have beliefs or consciousness

They are highly sophisticated prediction systems.

### Why Responses Can Differ Each Time

When asking the same question multiple times, responses may vary because:

- The model is not retrieving a stored answer.
- It generates a new response every time.
- Output is based on probabilities, not absolute truth.

## Importance of Training Data

### Key Principle

**Garbage In → Garbage Out**

- The quality of an LLM depends heavily on its training data.

### Good Training Data Leads To

- Better accuracy
- More reliable responses
- Better reasoning patterns

### Poor Training Data Leads To

- Biases
- Inaccuracies
- Hallucinations
- Misleading answers

**Training matters a lot**

## Training LLMs is Extremely Expensive

Training a large model requires:

### Massive Compute Resources

- Thousands of GPUs
- Weeks or months of training
- Large-scale infrastructure

### Result

Only a small number of companies can afford to train frontier models from scratch.

> Just as developers use databases without building their own database engines, they can use LLMs without training them.
